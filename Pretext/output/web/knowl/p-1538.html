The set of such \(n\)-tuples is the so-called \(n\)-dimensional space, often denoted by \({\mathbb R}^n\text{.}\) Sometimes we call this the \(n\)-dimensional euclidean space  2 . In two dimensions, \({\mathbb R}^2\) is called the cartesian plane  4 . Each such \(n\)-tuple represents a point in the \(n\)-dimensional space. For example, the point \((1,2)\) in the plane \({\mathbb R}^2\) is one unit to the right and two units up from the origin.
