Clearly these new equations have the same solutions \(x_1,x_2,x_3\text{.}\) A second operation is that we can multiply an equation by a nonzero number. For example, we multiply the third equation in (6.3.1) by 3:
\begin{equation*} \begin{aligned} 2 x_1 + 2 x_2 + 2 x_3 \amp = 2 , \\ x_1 + x_2 + 3 x_3 \amp = 5 , \\ 3 x_1 + 12 x_2 + 3 x_3 \amp = 30 . \end{aligned} \end{equation*}
