Finally we can add a multiple of one equation to another equation. For example, we add 3 times the third equation in (6.3.1) to the second equation:
\begin{equation*} \begin{aligned} 2 x_1 + 2 x_2 + 2 x_3 \amp = 2 , \\ (1+3) x_1 + (1+12) x_2 + (3+3) x_3 \amp = 5+30 , \\ x_1 + 4 x_2 + x_3 \amp = 10 . \end{aligned} \end{equation*}
