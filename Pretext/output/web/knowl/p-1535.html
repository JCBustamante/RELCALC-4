As motivation, suppose we wish to solve
\begin{equation*} \begin{aligned} \amp x-y = 2 , \\ \amp 2x+y = 4 , \end{aligned} \end{equation*}
for \(x\) and \(y\text{,}\) that is, find numbers \(x\) and \(y\) such that the two equations are satisfied. Let us perhaps start by adding the equations together to find
\begin{equation*} x+2x-y+y = 2+4, \qquad \text{ or } \qquad 3x = 6\text{.} \end{equation*}
