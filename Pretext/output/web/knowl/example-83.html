Example 6.5.7.

Consider the vectors \((1,2,-1)\text{,}\) and \((0,5,-2)\) and call \(S\) the span of the two vectors. Let us find an orthogonal basis of \(S\text{:}\)
\begin{align*} \vec{w}_1 \amp = (1,2,-1) ,\\ \vec{w}_2 \amp = (0,5,-2) - \operatorname{proj}_{(1,2,-1)}\bigl((0,2,-2)\bigr)\\ \amp = (0,1,-1) - \frac{\langle (0,5,-2), (1,2,-1) \rangle}{ \langle (1,2,-1), (1,2,-1) \rangle } (1,2,-1) = (0,5,-2) - 2 (1,2,-1) = (-2,1,0) \text{.} \end{align*}
So \((1,2,-1)\) and \((-2,1,0)\) span \(S\) and are orthogonal. Let us check: \((1,2,-1) \cdot (-2,1,0) = 0\text{.}\)
Suppose we wish to find an orthonormal basis, not just an orthogonal one. Well, we simply make the vectors into unit vectors by dividing them by their magnitude. The two vectors making up the orthonormal basis of \(S\) are:
\begin{equation*} \frac{1}{\sqrt{6}} (1,2,-1) = \left( \frac{1}{\sqrt{6}}, \frac{2}{\sqrt{6}}, \frac{-1}{\sqrt{6}} \right) , \qquad \frac{1}{\sqrt{5}} (-2,1,0) = \left( \frac{-2}{\sqrt{5}}, \frac{1}{\sqrt{5}}, 0 \right)\text{.} \end{equation*}
