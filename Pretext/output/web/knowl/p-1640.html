Let us see how the matrix works on a smaller example,
\begin{equation*} \begin{bmatrix} a_{11} \amp a_{12} \\ a_{21} \amp a_{22} \end{bmatrix} \begin{bmatrix} 1 \amp 0 \\ 0 \amp 1 \end{bmatrix} = \begin{bmatrix} a_{11} \cdot 1 + a_{12} \cdot 0 \amp \amp a_{11} \cdot 0 + a_{12} \cdot 1 \\ a_{21} \cdot 1 + a_{22} \cdot 0 \amp \amp a_{21} \cdot 0 + a_{22} \cdot 1 \end{bmatrix} = \begin{bmatrix} a_{11} \amp a_{12} \\ a_{21} \amp a_{22} \end{bmatrix}\text{.} \end{equation*}
