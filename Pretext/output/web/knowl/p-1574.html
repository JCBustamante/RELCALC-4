As a linear mapping \(M \colon {\mathbb R}^2 \to {\mathbb R}^2\) takes \(\vec{e}_1 = \left[ \begin{matrix}1 \\ 0 \end{matrix} \right]\) to \(\left[ \begin{matrix}1 \\ 3 \end{matrix} \right]\) and \(\vec{e}_2 = \left[ \begin{matrix}0 \\ 1 \end{matrix} \right]\) to \(\left[ \begin{matrix}2 \\ 4 \end{matrix} \right]\text{.}\) In other words,
\begin{equation*} M \vec{e}_1 = \begin{bmatrix} 1 \amp 2 \\ 3 \amp 4 \end{bmatrix} \begin{bmatrix} 1 \\ 0 \end{bmatrix} = \begin{bmatrix} 1 \\ 3 \end{bmatrix}, \qquad \text{ and } \qquad M \vec{e}_2 = \begin{bmatrix} 1 \amp 2 \\ 3 \amp 4 \end{bmatrix} \begin{bmatrix} 0 \\ 1 \end{bmatrix} = \begin{bmatrix} 2 \\ 4 \end{bmatrix}\text{.} \end{equation*}
