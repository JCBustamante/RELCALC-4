As we said a vector is a direction and a magnitude. Magnitude is easy to represent, it is just a number. The direction is usually given by a vector with magnitude one. We call such a vector a unit vector . That is, \(\vec{u}\) is a unit vector when \(\lVert \vec{u} \rVert = 1\text{.}\) For example, the vectors \((1,0)\text{,}\) \((\nicefrac{1}{\sqrt{2}},\nicefrac{1}{\sqrt{2}})\text{,}\) and \((0,-1)\) are all unit vectors.
