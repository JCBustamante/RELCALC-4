Let \({\mathbb R}^3\) represent the space of quadratic polynomials in \(t\text{:}\) a point \((a_0,a_1,a_2)\) in \({\mathbb R}^3\) represents the polynomial \(a_0 + a_1 t + a_2 t^2\text{.}\) Consider the derivative \(\frac{d}{dt}\) as a mapping of \({\mathbb R}^3\) to \({\mathbb R}^3\text{,}\) and note that \(\frac{d}{dt}\) is linear. Write down \(\frac{d}{dt}\) as a \(3 \times 3\) matrix.
