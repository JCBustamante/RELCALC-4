The first thing is to write the equations in a more compact manner. Given
\begin{equation*} A \vec{x} = \vec{b}\text{,} \end{equation*}
we write down the so-called augmented matrix
\begin{equation*} [ A ~|~ \vec{b} ]\text{,} \end{equation*}
where the vertical line is just a marker for us to know where the “right-hand side” of the equation starts. For example, for the system (6.3.1) the augmented matrix is
\begin{equation*} \left[ \begin{array}{ccc|c} 2 \amp 2 \amp 2 \amp 2 \\ 1 \amp 1 \amp 3 \amp 5 \\ 1 \amp 4 \amp 1 \amp 10 \end{array} \right]\text{.} \end{equation*}
