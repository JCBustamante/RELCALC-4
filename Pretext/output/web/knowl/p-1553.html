We put all of these operations together to work out more complicated expressions. Let us compute a small example:
\begin{equation*} 3 \begin{bmatrix} 1 \\ 2 \end{bmatrix} + 2 \begin{bmatrix} -4 \\ -1 \end{bmatrix} - 3 \begin{bmatrix} -2 \\ 2 \end{bmatrix} = \begin{bmatrix} 3(1)+2(-4)-3(-2) \\ 3(2)+2(-1)-3(2) \end{bmatrix} = \begin{bmatrix} 1 \\ -2 \end{bmatrix}\text{.} \end{equation*}
