Now how does a matrix \(A\) relate to a linear mapping? Well a matrix tells you where certain special vectors go. Let's give a name to those certain vectors. The standard basis vectors of \({\mathbb R}^n\) are
\begin{equation*} \vec{e}_1 = \begin{bmatrix} 1 \\ 0 \\ 0 \\ \vdots \\ 0 \end{bmatrix} , \qquad \vec{e}_2 = \begin{bmatrix} 0 \\ 1 \\ 0 \\ \vdots \\ 0 \end{bmatrix} , \qquad \vec{e}_3 = \begin{bmatrix} 0 \\ 0 \\ 1 \\ \vdots \\ 0 \end{bmatrix} , \qquad \cdots , \qquad \vec{e}_n = \begin{bmatrix} 0 \\ 0 \\ 0 \\ \vdots \\ 1 \end{bmatrix}\text{.} \end{equation*}
