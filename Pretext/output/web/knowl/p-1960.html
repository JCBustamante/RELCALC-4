There are a number of ways to define the determinant for an \(n \times n\) matrix. Let us use the so-called cofactor expansion . We define \(A_{ij}\) as the matrix \(A\) with the \(i^{\text{ th } }\) row and the \(j^{\text{ th } }\) column deleted. For example, if
\begin{equation*} \text{ If } \qquad A = \begin{bmatrix} 1 \amp 2 \amp 3 \\ 4 \amp 5 \amp 6 \\ 7 \amp 8 \amp 9 \end{bmatrix} , \qquad \text{ then } \qquad A_{12} = \begin{bmatrix} 4 \amp 6 \\ 7 \amp 9 \end{bmatrix} \qquad \text{ and } \qquad A_{23} = \begin{bmatrix} 1 \amp 2 \\ 7 \amp 8 \end{bmatrix}\text{.} \end{equation*}
